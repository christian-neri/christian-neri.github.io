Home · Work · Radioactive

Oscar®-nominated Rosamund Pike stars as Marie Curie in Studiocanal's Radioactive. One month before release, COVID-19 shut cinemas worldwide — the digital experience had to carry narrative momentum through an unprecedented pause.

Studio

Studiocanal

Challenge

Paused theatrical window

Goal

Sustain audience curiosity

Context

A prestige biopic launch typically funnels attention through theatrical buzz, influencer beats, and interactive digital extensions. When physical screenings halted, Radioactive's flagship site evolved into the primary stage for sustaining emotional connection with the film's feminist-science story.

Goal

Maintain engagement between the paused campaign and delayed premiere milestones. The experience leaned on richly art-directed modules — trailer beats, exploratory “Streets of Paris” journeys, and character-centric storytelling vignettes tuned for bingeable mobile sessions.

Approach

Rolling narrative sections balanced editorial typography with tactile UI overlays that mirror the kinetic key art palette. Lightweight interactive layers prolonged dwell time without asking audiences to hurdle heavy UI — optimizing for spontaneous discovery on handheld screens during lockdown-era scroll sessions.

Experience frames

Campaign-critical screens spanning mobile canvases, trailer beats, tertiary storytelling paths, and artful character studies.
